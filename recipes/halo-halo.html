Halo-halo

Halo-Halo is a popular Filipino dessert that’s as colorful as it is delicious. The name "halo-halo" translates to "mix-mix" in English, which perfectly describes the process of combining various ingredients to create this refreshing treat. Traditionally, halo-halo consists of a mixture of sweetened fruits, jellies, and beans, topped with finely shaved ice, evaporated milk, and a scoop of ice cream. It’s a delightful blend of textures and flavors, from the sweetness of the fruits to the creaminess of the ice cream, making it a perfect dessert for hot days.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup sweetened red beans
  • 1/4 cup sweetened white beans
  • 1/4 cup nata de coco (coconut gel)
  • 1/4 cup kaong (sugar palm fruit)
  • 1/4 cup macapuno (coconut strips)
  • 1/4 cup sweetened saba bananas (or plantains), sliced
  • 1/4 cup sweetened jackfruit (langka), sliced
  • 1/4 cup sweetened purple yam (ube halaya)
  • 1/2 cup jellies or gulaman, cut into cubes
  • 2 cups finely shaved ice
  • 1/2 cup evaporated milk
  • 2 scoops ube or vanilla ice cream
  • Leche flan (optional)
  • Pinipig (toasted rice flakes) for garnish

Steps

  1. Prepare the Base: In a tall glass or bowl, layer the sweetened red beans, white beans, nata de coco, kaong, macapuno, sweetened saba bananas, and sweetened jackfruit.
  2. Add Jellies: Place the jellies or gulaman on top of the fruit and bean layers.
  3. Top with Shaved Ice: Add a generous amount of finely shaved ice over the layers of ingredients, filling the glass or bowl.
  4. Pour the Milk: Drizzle evaporated milk over the shaved ice until it is partially soaked.
  5. Add the Ice Cream: Place a scoop of ube or vanilla ice cream on top of the shaved ice.
  6. Garnish: Optionally, add a slice of leche flan on top of the ice cream and sprinkle with pinipig (toasted rice flakes) for extra texture.
  7. Serve: Serve the Halo-Halo immediately with a long spoon. Mix all the ingredients together before eating to enjoy the full spectrum of flavors and textures.
